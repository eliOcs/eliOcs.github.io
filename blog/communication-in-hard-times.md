# Communicating in hard times

- The 2021 SaaS hypergrowth market popped in 2023, we weren’t able to hit our
  growth targets and the funding market dried so we had to cut costs. In May
  2023 we unfortunately had to let go of 4 people in the development/engineering
  team.
- We did everything in our hand to reduce the amount of people that we let go.
  We went through all our company tool stack to cut down on our tool costs. We
  reduced our infrastructure costs, eg: moving out of Auth0. We offered
  employees to reduce their working hours.
- For the people we let go we made sure they got a good severance package, we
  wrote recommendation letters and we used our network to help them get new jobs
- We acknowledged our mistake we were unable to execute the strategy to achieve
  our growth goals
- I needed to select the team members which were must haves to keep our product
  operational and team functional
- Communicate the decision to each of the people we let go.
- Properly communicate: honest, transparent and clear. Share enough context for
  the team to understand our decision: our growth numbers were lower than we
  predicted, the funding market dried up and the way forward was to achieve
  profitability. We did everything we could think of to reduce the people we let
  go. We proved kindness in our actions. Shared our company financial
  numbers/state with the rest of the company. Explain the current economic
  context and how it suddenly shifted.
- Afterwards I communicated to the team the expectations after the lay offs was
  to concentrate on reliability and keeping things running and therefore we will
  have to lower the speed of development of new features. To clearly state that
  we were not going to continue doing the same amount of work with less people.
- We explained how we were going to redistribute the responsibilities of the
  people we let go so everyone was on the same page and clear about who owned
  what.
- As the months went by we clearly showed our profitability target and how we
  were evolving into it
- No one left the team after the layoffs, we managed to successfully retain the
  key players
- Satisfaction metrics in engineering kept high even after layoffs 90+
  satisfaction scores Feature throughput lowered but we managed to hit
  profitability
